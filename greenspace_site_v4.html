Urban Paradox

High-SVI urban tracts are more likely to have a park within 800m

5
Double Deprivation
636,293
Residents in greenspace deserts: high vulnerability AND no walkable park
152 tracts (10.4%) simultaneously face SVI ≥ 0.75 and no public park within 800m. These tracts average 36.2% poverty, 80.9% minority population, and just 1.0% usable greenspace. Together they house 636,293 residents facing compounding environmental disadvantage.
6
Water Quality · EPD
60%
60% of assessed Atlanta-area streams are impaired
Of 490 EPD-assessed stream reaches, 294 (60%) are "Not Supporting" their designated use. Common causes: E. coli, biological impairment, habitat degradation. Even where communities have physical access to waterways, those waterways may not provide safe recreational or ecological benefit — a second layer of environmental inequity.
Methods

How we built this

A spatial equity analysis integrating four independent data sources across Atlanta's 21-county metropolitan area.

🛰️
Greenspace Data
3,860 greenspace polygons across 21 Georgia counties. Public Parks, Community Open Spaces, and Recreation Facilities are rendered as actual polygon boundaries. Golf courses excluded from equity analysis.
💧
EPD Water Quality
Georgia EPD 305(b)/303(d) integrated assessment. 490 stream reaches and 6 lakes for Atlanta MSA, classified as Supporting, Not Supporting, or Assessment Pending their designated use (primarily fishing and swimming).
🗂️
Census Tract Geography
2025 TIGER/Line tract boundaries for Georgia. Spatial overlay in EPSG:2240. Tract geometries simplified at 100m tolerance; greenspace polygons simplified at ~55m tolerance for web performance.
📊
CDC Social Vulnerability Index
2022 SVI for Georgia: RPL_THEMES composite, four sub-theme ranks, and demographics including poverty rate, minority %, Black %, Hispanic %. Tracts with SVI=−999 excluded (n=10).
📍
Live 800m Access Ring
Click anywhere → real-time 800m radius circle at that exact point. Sidebar counts greenspaces within the ring by type using Haversine distance. Per-click calculation, not pre-computed by tract centroid.
📈
Statistical Models
OLS with HC3 robust standard errors. Logistic regression for binary 800m access. Tract water quality = % of nearby EPD reaches classified as impaired. Python: statsmodels, geopandas.
Discussion

How this analysis could be extended

Current limitations and the steps that would complete this equity framework.

01
Street-network walking distance
The 800m ring is Euclidean. Real walkability in car-dependent suburban Atlanta depends on street connectivity — a park 600m away may require a 1.5km detour. OSMnx walking isochrones would make access measurement far more realistic.
02
Water quality × SVI regression
With EPD waterway data integrated, the next step is formally testing whether % impaired reaches correlates with SVI after controlling for urbanization — connecting water quality to the equity framework.
03
Park quality and amenity data
Two parks 800m away can be very different. Trust for Public Land ParkScore or Google Places amenities would shift from presence to quality — a more meaningful equity metric.
04
Population-weighted coverage
5% coverage in a 10,000-person urban tract vs. a 500-person rural tract are very different. Acres-per-resident would reduce rural overrepresentation in the current tract-area denominator.
05
Heat stress overlay
Landsat LST data would connect greenspace deprivation to direct public health outcomes, strengthening the policy argument for targeted investment in double-deprivation tracts.
06
Temporal greenspace change
Historical NDVI time series would reveal whether gentrification brings greenspace gains to formerly vulnerable tracts — the "green gentrification" pattern documented in other US cities.
